How the self-extract budget gate works (the GREEN gate for THIS plan):
- `bash tests/D-reviewer-budget.sh` (no args) and `bash tests/D-security-reviewer-budget.sh` (no args)
  awk-range the holistic worked example: from the line beginning the blockquoted Critical section
  through the first NON-blockquote line, strip the leading blockquote `>` + wrapping backticks, then parse.
- Parser: SEV_HEADERS matches the four severity headers on their own line; FINDING_RE matches a numbered
  finding line `N. <file>:<line>: ...` (security additionally requires the `[<tag>]` bracket).
- Per-finding body span = the line AFTER stripping the `N. <file>:<line>: ` (plus `[<tag>] ` for security)
  prefix. Counted with `wc -w`. Default cap PER_ENTRY_CAP=28. Security: a body containing a `[CVE`/`[GHSA`/`[CWE`
  bracket escalates to AUTO_CLARITY_CAP=75.
- `<verify_request ...>` lines are SKIPPED (not findings, not counted).
- `### Per-finding validation` entries (prefix `Validation of Finding N:`) do NOT match FINDING_RE, so the
  budget loop does NOT count them -- this is exactly why routing rationale there removes it from the
  per-finding span (FIX-1). The .sh fixture does NOT separately enforce the 60w PFV cap; that 60w cap is a
  CONTRACT cap verified by manual word count, not by the fixture.
- MIN_FINDINGS=5 anti-vacuous floor. The reviewer holistic example has 7 findings; security has 6.
  Keep finding counts >=5 (preserve current counts) so the guard does not fire on the self-extract.
- REQUIRED sections asserted present: `### Cross-Cutting Patterns` (reviewer) / `### Threat Patterns` (security).
- `--self-test` synthesizes a zero-finding input and MUST exit 1 (anti-vacuous guard fires by design).
