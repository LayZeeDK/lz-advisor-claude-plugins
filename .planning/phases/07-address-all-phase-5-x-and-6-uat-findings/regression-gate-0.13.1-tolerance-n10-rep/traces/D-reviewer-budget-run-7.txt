### Per-finding validation

The regex `/<[^>]*>/g` does not address `javascript:` href schemes, surviving `on*` handlers when input lacks `>`, double-encoded payloads, or SVG/MathML vectors. A wrong-named primitive is worse than no primitive -- it is load-bearing under the Severity Classification's "security flaw that affects users in normal operation" clause.

Validation of Finding 5: Raised to Important. Type mismatch between `parseId: number | null` and `User.id: string` is not stylistic -- the first caller wiring `parseId(route.params.id)` into a `User` lookup will get a TS2345 error, or worse, a runtime mismatch if `as any` papers over it.
