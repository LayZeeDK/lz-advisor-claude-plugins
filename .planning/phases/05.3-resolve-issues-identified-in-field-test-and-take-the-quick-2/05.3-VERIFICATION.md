## Key Findings (worth carrying forward)

1. **Context Trust Contract works as designed.** Post-fix advisor consultations used 0 internal tool calls in 3 of 4 skills. The advisor trusts the packaged context (D-06, D-07) and synthesizes in a single turn.
2. **`execute` skill shows the largest consultation efficiency gain.** Advisor turn-messages dropped from 5 (baseline) to 1 (post-fix), and executor tool calls dropped from 61 to 35. The packaging reference is load-bearing for the consult-implement loop.
3. **`security-review` is a role-calibration success case.** Post-fix did 2 targeted Read/Glob calls to verify executor claims and revised Finding 1 severity from Medium to Informational after confirming the file was already gitignored. D-08's "efficient batched verification" pattern materialized as intended.
4. **Opus 4.7 tone is not regressing tact.** UAT 4 Likert rubric shows 5/6 dimensions improved (directness, severity-consistency, remediation specificity); 0/6 dimensions dropped. The 4.7 release-note warning about "more direct tone blunting tact" did not materialize in this phase's reviewer/security-reviewer runs.
5. **stream-json format is CLI-session-shaped, not SDK-shaped.** Plan 03 discovered this; the `parse-trace.mjs` update is forward-compatible and handled all 8 Plan 04 runs cleanly.
6. **Word-count caps are not strict gates.** Post-fix responses exceeded the 100w (advisor) and 300w (reviewer) caps by 9-32%, but UAT-RESULTS.md documents this as informational. Follow-up options: raise caps, exclude fenced code from word count, or keep as aspirational targets.
