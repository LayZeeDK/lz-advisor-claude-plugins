Cross-Cutting Patterns

Findings 1 and 2 share a root cause: the build pipeline treats documentation.json as a runtime executor artifact while preview.ts treats it as a compile-time module. This mismatch is the load-bearing issue -- Finding 1 is the failure mode, Finding 2 is the structural smell that makes the coupling fragile (output path is implicit, not declared as an Nx target output). A cleaner design would model compodoc generation as its own Nx target with explicit outputs, then declare dependsOn: ["compodoc"] on storybook and build-storybook. This would make the artifact tracked by Nx's input/output graph, enable caching, and let lint/typecheck steps depend on it explicitly rather than racing against executor side effects.

One item not in your packaged findings worth flagging: compodocArgs uses workspace-relative paths (packages/ngx-smart-components/...) inside a project-scoped project.json. This works because the Storybook executor runs from workspace root, but it couples the project config to its current location -- moving the package would silently break compodoc. Not severe, but worth a comment in project.json if you keep the current structure.
